The Road Thus Far
What have I been doing professionally for the last five years?
Five years ago, I was given my first chance by someone who became a coach and inspiration for me. I started my career as a junior developer with a lot of personal experience but no professional experience. I dove in head first and was handed a low-risk project that could recover from a junior developer's learning curve. It was a lot to take in at once and I was afraid at times that I was in over my head, but I was not turning back. The challenge and the constant motiviation to do better and the urgency to deliver drove me to stop making excuses and to push and push until I finally grasped the project I was handed and began to run through a list of outstanding bugs and enhancement requests that needed attention. Once I instilled some confidence in management, I was given more projects to own and opportunities to learn and grow. I continued to work on these projects for the next couple of years while I began to be get opportunities to get involved in the hiring process and begin to recruit a team to work with me on various projects. I have had the fortune of watching those individuals grow and face their challenges while being able to provide support where appropriate and their growth has allowed me to lean on their growing knowledge and stay engaged on the development side of the business. In the last year I provided a proof of concept to our upper management that was looked at and given a chance. I ended up investing all of my free time for months into this project and then got clearance to transform it into a project within our organization and work on it during working hours as well. In the end, I architected, developed, and implemented a pure javascript library that allowed for a customizable data grid to seamlessly scroll through millions of records and allow custom control integration. I was able to learn a ton about optomization and how to best design interactions between a client and server to streamline data display for record sets of huge scale.
